1. The Pilgrim of the Outer Shrines
You were raised in a wandering spiritual order that traveled between ancient Crux shrines scattered across the frontier worlds. Your people believed the shrines were alive — or at least listening. You never fully accepted their teachings, but you learned discipline, ritual, and how to read the subtle “moods” of the land. When the order fractured over a prophecy, you walked away, unsure whether you were abandoning destiny or finally choosing your own.

2. The Corporate Defector
You grew up inside a megacorp arcology where everything — education, food, even your future — was predetermined by your aptitude scores. You excelled, but the corporation’s expectations became a cage. After discovering a classified project involving Crux manipulation and forced augmentation, you fled. Now you live with the knowledge that the corp wants you back… either for your skills or for what you know.

3. The Frontier Scrap‑Dweller
Your early life was spent in a settlement built from population ships, old mining rigs, and whatever tech could be scavenged from the wastes. You learned to survive by improvisation: patching gear with scrap, bartering with travelers, and avoiding the creatures that roamed the wild places. When a mysterious object fell from the sky and pulsed with Crux energy, everything changed — and you left home to understand why it reacted to you.

4. The Exiled Heir
You were born into a respected lineage — political, noble, or tribal depending on species — but internal conflict forced you out. Some say you were framed. Others say you fled responsibility. You carry a token of your former life, a reminder of what you lost or what you escaped. Whether you seek redemption, revenge, or freedom is something you haven’t fully decided.

5. The War‑Born Survivor
Your childhood was shaped by a conflict that spanned multiple species and territories. You weren’t a soldier, but you lived in the aftermath: ruined cities, refugee caravans, and the uneasy alliances that followed. You learned to fight because you had to. You learned to heal because someone needed to. Now the war is over, but the skills — and the scars — remain.

6. The Crux‑Touched Wanderer
You were found wandering near a Crux anomaly — a rift, a storm, a crystalline growth — with no memory of how you got there. Some believe you were altered by it. Others think you were meant to die there. You’ve pieced together fragments of your past, but the anomaly still calls to you in dreams. Whether it’s a warning or an invitation is unclear.

7. The Scholar of Lost Worlds
You were raised in a research enclave dedicated to studying extinct civilizations and forgotten species. Your mentors taught you languages, symbols, and the art of reading history from ruins. But when your team uncovered a relic that reacted violently to Crux energy, the enclave sealed the site and forbade further study. You left, unwilling to let the truth be buried.

8. The Street‑Raised Ghost
You grew up in the underlevels of a massive city‑station — a place where species mixed, laws blurred, and survival depended on staying unnoticed. You learned to move quietly, talk fast, and trust slowly. A single event pushed you out of that life: a betrayal, a job gone wrong, or a moment of compassion you couldn’t ignore. Now you walk between worlds, never fully belonging to any of them.

9. The Former Devotee of the Machine Cult
Your early years were spent among a sect that believed machines, nanites, and Crux‑tech were divine expressions of cosmic order. You were trained to maintain sacred engines, interpret system “prophecies,” and listen for the hum of truth. But when you witnessed corruption within the cult — or a revelation that contradicted its teachings — you left. Some members still consider you chosen. Others consider you a heretic.

10. The Genetic Outlier
You were born with traits your species rarely exhibits — unusual Crux affinity, a mutation, a hybrid lineage, or something stranger. Scientists, shamans, or elders studied you, depending on your culture. Some feared you. Some revered you. You left home to escape expectations, or to understand what you truly are. The universe feels like it’s watching you… or waiting for you.
